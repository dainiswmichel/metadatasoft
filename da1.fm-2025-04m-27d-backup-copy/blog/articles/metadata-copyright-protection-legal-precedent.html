Metadata Copyright Protection Gets Big Win in Court

Dainis W. Michel April 13, 2025 3 min read

In a ruling with profound implications for digital creators, a federal court has established that removing metadata from digital images constitutes a violation of copyright management information protections under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This landmark legal precedent directly supports DA1's mission to preserve and protect creator attribution.

"The court found that metadata embedded in digital files constitutes 'copyright management information' under 17 U.S.C. § 1202, making its removal or alteration a violation of federal law."

— PetaPixel, reporting on the Philpot v. WOS, Inc. case

A Legal Foundation for Attribution Rights

The case of Philpot v. WOS, Inc. establishes several critical precedents that strengthen creators' legal position regarding metadata:

  • Metadata is legally protected as copyright management information
  • Platforms and publishers have an obligation to preserve this information
  • Statutory damages are available for metadata stripping violations
  • Intent is not required for liability—systems that automatically strip metadata create legal exposure
  • Attribution is recognized as a legally protected right worth preserving

This ruling transforms metadata from a technical nicety into a legally protected component of creative works. Organizations that build systems that strip metadata now face potential legal liability under federal law.

From Technical to Legal Protection

The court's ruling represents a significant shift in how the law views metadata—transforming it from a purely technical consideration to a legally protected aspect of creative works. This shift aligns perfectly with DA1's vision that attribution information deserves the same protection and persistence as the content itself.

What makes this ruling particularly powerful is that it doesn't require new legislation—it interprets existing DMCA provisions to cover metadata protection. This means the legal foundation for metadata preservation already exists in most jurisdictions, even if awareness and enforcement are still developing.

For creators, this means the legal system is increasingly recognizing what DA1 has built its technology around: attribution is a fundamental right that deserves both technical and legal protection.
