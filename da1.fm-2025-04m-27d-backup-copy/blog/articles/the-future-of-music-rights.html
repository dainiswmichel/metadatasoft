The Future of Music Rights in a Digital Ecosystem

Dainis W. Michel April 1, 2025 8 min read

Traditional music rights management is reaching its breaking point. With streaming platforms processing billions of micro-transactions monthly, the outdated systems of tracking, reporting, and paying artists are collapsing under their own weight. DA1 offers a revolutionary approach that leverages blockchain technology and smart contracts to create a more transparent, efficient, and fair ecosystem for all stakeholders.

The Broken State of Music Rights

The current music rights landscape is characterized by fragmentation, opacity, and inefficiency. When a single song is streamed, it can potentially trigger payments to multiple rights holders:

  • Songwriters (composers and lyricists)
  • Publishers who represent songwriters
  • Recording artists (performers)
  • Record labels who finance and distribute recordings
  • Producers who help create the recordings
  • Session musicians who played on the recordings

However, tracking these payments is extraordinarily complex. Many rights holders report waiting 12-24 months to receive streaming royalties, with some payments never arriving at all due to:

  1. Insufficient metadata that fails to properly identify rights holders
  2. Siloed databases that don't communicate effectively across platforms
  3. Manual reconciliation processes that introduce errors and delays
  4. Lack of transparency in how payments are calculated and distributed

"The music industry's infrastructure is stuck in the pre-internet era, even as consumption has moved fully into the digital space."

— Vickie Nauman, CrossBorderWorks

Blockchain as the Foundation for Change

Blockchain technology provides a fundamental solution to many of these challenges by creating:

  • A single source of truth for ownership and rights information
  • Immutable records that prevent retroactive changes without consent
  • Transparent transactions that all stakeholders can verify
  • Automated payments through smart contracts

Smart Contracts and Rights Administration

Smart contracts—self-executing agreements with the terms directly written into code—are particularly powerful for music rights. They enable:

  • Instant payment splitting when a work is used (streaming, downloads, licensing)
  • Automatic enforcement of contractual terms without middlemen
  • Reduced administration costs by eliminating manual reconciliation
  • Adaptable rights management as career relationships evolve

DA1's Integrated Rights Management System

Our approach at DA1 goes beyond simply applying blockchain to the current broken system. We've reimagined rights management from the ground up:

1. Universal Rights Identifier (URI)

Every creative work in the DA1 ecosystem receives a Universal Rights Identifier that:

  • Links all versions and formats of a work (master recordings, stems, demos, etc.)
  • Maintains connections to all related rights documents
  • Preserves the full history of rights transfers and licenses
  • Integrates with existing identifiers (ISRC, ISWC, UPC, etc.)

2. Embedded Rights Data

Rather than storing rights information in disconnected databases, DA1 embeds critical rights data directly in the media files themselves:

  • Rights data travels with the content, regardless of platform
  • Attribution remains intact even when files are copied or converted
  • Platforms can verify rights status before allowing uploads
  • Usage tracking becomes automated and accurate

3. Tiered Smart Contracts

Our tiered smart contract system handles different levels of rights complexity:

  • Standard contracts for common scenarios (straight royalty splits, standard terms)
  • Custom contracts for complex relationships (advances, recoupment, graduated rates)
  • Legacy integration for works with pre-existing contracts
  • Orphan works protection to ensure creators can claim their rights
