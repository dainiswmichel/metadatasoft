The State of Metadata in 2022: A Cross-Industry Crisis

Dainis W. Michel April 23, 2025 3 min read

A comprehensive industry analysis by Dataversity reveals a startling projection: by 2025, an estimated 85% of all data will have incorrect or insufficient metadata, creating information chaos across virtually every industry and sector. This cross-industry crisis requires the kind of structural solution that DA1's metametadata system provides.

"The metadata crisis spans well beyond creative industries into every sector that depends on digital information. Without structural solutions, enterprises face a future where the majority of their data assets become effectively unfindable, unusable, and untrustable."

— Dataversity, "The State of Metadata in 2022"

A Universal Digital Problem

What makes the Dataversity analysis particularly valuable is its recognition that metadata problems transcend any single industry or content type. The report identifies metadata deficiencies affecting virtually every sector:

  • Healthcare where patient records lose critical context
  • Financial services where transaction data loses compliance information
  • Manufacturing where product specifications become disconnected from components
  • Retail where customer data loses consent information
  • Energy where sensor data becomes uninterpretable without context

This universal nature of the metadata crisis demonstrates that the issue isn't merely a concern for media and creative industries—it represents a fundamental flaw in how digital information is structured and maintained across all domains.

DA1's Cross-Industry Solution

  • Content-embedded contextual information that remains persistently connected

The Economic Imperative

Dataversity's analysis puts concrete numbers on the cost of metadata failures across industries:

  • 30-35% of time wasted by knowledge workers searching for information
  • 20% of data assets becoming effectively orphaned each year
  • $12.9 million average annual cost per enterprise just from poor data quality
  • 14.2 hours per week spent by data professionals just on metadata reconciliation

These economic impacts make clear that metadata isn't just a technical concern—it's a business imperative affecting productivity, compliance, and bottom-line performance across every industry.

As the volume and complexity of data continue to grow exponentially, the metadata crisis will only worsen unless structural solutions like DA1's metametadata approach are implemented at scale.
