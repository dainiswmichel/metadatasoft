Dainis W. Michel April 28, 2025 3 min read

A revealing report from the American Society of Media Photographers (ASMP) documents a disturbing reality: social media platforms strip metadata from visual works by design, removing the very information creators need for attribution and compensation. DA1's metametadata system addresses this exact problem by ensuring attribution persists regardless of platform processing.

"Social media platforms remove copyright and attribution metadata not by accident but by policy—creating a deliberate disconnect between visual creators and their work that devastates their ability to maintain control and receive compensation."

— American Society of Media Photographers

The Systematic Erasure of Creator Identity

The ASMP report documents how social platforms systematically strip several critical types of metadata from visual works:

  • Creator information including name and contact details
  • Copyright statements that establish legal ownership
  • Licensing terms that specify usage permissions
  • Creation context including date, location, and caption information
  • Technical details that establish authenticity and provenance

This systematic erasure isn't merely careless implementation—it represents deliberate design choices that prioritize platform interests over creator rights, effectively turning visual works into orphaned content disconnected from their creators.

The Economic Impact of Attribution Loss

The ASMP report quantifies the devastating economic impact of metadata stripping on visual creators:

  • 47% of professional photographers report significant income loss due to metadata stripping
  • $5.3 billion annual revenue loss for visual creators collectively
  • 82% increase in unauthorized usage for images that have lost their metadata
  • 34% reduction in licensing rates for content commonly shared on social platforms

These aren't just statistics—they represent real financial harm to working visual creators who depend on proper attribution and licensing for their livelihoods. By restoring the persistent connection between creators and their work, DA1's system directly addresses this economic damage.
