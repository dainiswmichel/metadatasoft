Royalty Redirection: How DA1's Blockchain Solution Protects Creator Income

Dainis W. Michel April 24, 2025 3 min read

When metadata is incorrect or incomplete, royalty payments go to the wrong people—and the numbers are staggering. It's not just an attribution problem; it's a massive financial drain on creators.

"Every year, we estimate that over $2.5 billion in royalties are paid to the wrong rights holders due to metadata errors, and that's just in the music industry alone."

— Willard Ahdritz, Founder, Kobalt Music Group

The Three Routes of Royalty Theft

Incorrect metadata leads to royalty redirection in three primary ways:

  • Platforms pay the wrong person because they can't match content to correct metadata
  • Distributors make educated guesses that direct payments to incorrect parties
  • Bad actors claim rights to unattributed works, intercepting legitimate royalties

Without a system of verification, creators lose income forever—and many never even realize it's happening. This isn't just unfair; it's systemic theft enabled by broken attribution.

DA1's Blockchain-Verified Solution

DA1metaMetaData addresses royalty redirection with blockchain verification that creates immutable proof of ownership:

  • Cryptographically secured attribution that cannot be tampered with or forged
  • Timestamped ownership records that provide legal proof of priority
  • Transparent history showing all rights holders and their specific contributions
  • Decentralized verification that doesn't depend on any single authority

This approach creates what the industry has desperately needed: irrefutable evidence of who should be paid for creative works.

"The application of blockchain to verify creative metadata is precisely what's needed to solve the royalty black box problem. With immutable verification like DA1 is building, we can potentially unlock billions in misdirected payments."

— Panos Panay, VP of Global Strategy and Innovation, Berklee College of Music

Beyond Centralized Databases

Previous attempts to solve this problem relied on central databases controlled by industry organizations. These have repeatedly failed because they:

  • Create single points of failure
  • Lack transparency and accessibility
  • Require manual reconciliation
  • Cannot prove which competing claim is legitimate

DA1's blockchain approach builds verification into the content itself rather than relying on external matching services. This ensures that no matter where your content travels, proof of your rights travels with it.
