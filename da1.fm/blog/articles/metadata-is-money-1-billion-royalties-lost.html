Metadata Is Money: $1.5 Billion Royalties Lost Annually

Dainis W. Michel April 7, 2025 3 min read

According to Billboard's industry report, a staggering $1.5 billion in music royalties remains unpaid annually, trapped in what the industry calls "black boxes" because of metadata problems. Let that sink in—creators are missing out on over $4 million every day because the current system cannot reliably connect content to its creators.

"Missing, bad or inconsistent song metadata costs the music industry an estimated $25 million a day in lost revenue."

— The Verge

The Metadata Crisis Is a Financial Crisis

This isn't just a technical problem; it's a financial catastrophe for creators who depend on royalty payments to sustain their careers. When the metadata that identifies rightful owners becomes corrupted, disconnected, or incomplete, the money sits in limbo—often forever.

The current system fails because:

  • Platforms strip attribution data during distribution and formatting
  • No universal standard exists across the music ecosystem
  • Attribution chains break with each transfer between systems
  • Reconciliation is manual and nearly impossible at scale
