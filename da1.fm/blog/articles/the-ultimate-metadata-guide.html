The Metadata Hierarchy: What You Need to Know

Not all metadata is created equal. Understanding the hierarchy helps prioritize your workflow:

1. Core Identity Metadata

This identifies what your content fundamentally is:

  • ISRC (International Standard Recording Code) - Unique identifier for sound recordings
  • ISWC (International Standard Musical Work Code) - Identifies the underlying composition
  • UPC/EAN - Required for album/EP releases and retail
  • IPI/IPN (Interested Party Information) - Identifies creators and rights holders
  • Title, Version, Artist - The fundamental descriptors

The DA1 ecosystem simplifies this process by auto-generating and syncing these identifiers when you create your initial records.

Pro Tip

Keep track of your ISRCs in a spreadsheet with links to the master audio files. This simple practice prevents duplicate ISRC assignments and helps track your catalog's growth.

2. Rights and Ownership Metadata

This determines who gets paid and how:

  • Songwriter/composer splits - Percentages of publishing ownership
  • Recording copyright ownership - Who owns the master recordings
  • Publisher information - Affiliated publishing companies
  • PRO affiliations - Which performing rights organizations represent the writers
  • Licensing restrictions - Any territorial or usage limitations

3. Technical Audio Metadata

This provides critical information about the audio itself:

  • Format specifications - Sample rate, bit depth, file format
  • Duration and timing - Precise length, BPM, time signature
  • Production credits - Engineers, producers, studios
  • Recording dates - When the content was created and modified
  • DAW session information - Reference to source files and sessions

4. Descriptive & Discovery Metadata

This helps your audience find your music:

  • Genre and subgenre - Primary and secondary classifications
  • Moods and themes - Emotional qualities and subject matter
  • Instrumentation - Key instruments featured
  • Lyrical content - Keywords from lyrics, language information
  • Similar artists - Relational connections to other creators

Practical Implementation: Your Metadata Workflow

Step 1: Capture Metadata at Source

Begin your metadata journey at the point of creation:

  • Set up DAW templates with metadata fields embedded in session files
  • Document performers, instruments, and recording details during sessions
  • Tag stems and multi-tracks with consistent naming conventions
  • Save processing chains and plugin settings as part of your technical documentation

The DA1 Creator's Assistant plugin (available for all major DAWs) automatically captures this data during the creation process and prepares it for the next stage.

Step 2: Enrich During Post-Production

As your project develops, enrich your metadata:

  • Add descriptive tags like moods, genre, BPM, key, and energy level
  • Document final mix and mastering engineers
  • Define important sections with markers (verses, choruses, drops)
  • Record final technical specifications

Step 3: Complete for Distribution

Before release, ensure your comprehensive distribution package includes:

  • All core identifiers (ISRC, ISWC, UPC)
  • Complete artist and songwriter information with correct spellings
  • Publishing splits and rights management details
  • Release dates and territorial information
  • Cover art with embedded metadata
  • Promotional assets tagged with the same identifiers

Common Pitfall

Many artists focus only on the metadata required by their distributor's upload form. This minimal approach severely limits your music's potential. The DA1 distribution preparation tool identifies gaps in your metadata profile and suggests enrichments that will improve your music's discoverability.
