Music Industry Metadata Advocates (250)

Music Industry Leaders & Organizations

1

Michael Huppe - President and CEO of SoundExchange

Email: michael.huppe@soundexchange.com

Twitter: @michaelhuppe

As the leader of SoundExchange, Michael Huppe oversees the premier music tech organization administering sound recording licenses and distributing royalties. Under his leadership, SoundExchange has distributed billions in royalties to creators and developed robust systems for tracking and attributing music usage across platforms.

"We're living in a golden age of music. More music is being consumed than ever before, and to more listeners, through more services and platforms, and in more geographical regions."
2

Björn Ulvaeus - Songwriter, Musician (ABBA), President of CISAC

Email: bjorn@cisac.org

Björn Ulvaeus, best known as a member of ABBA, launched the Credits Due initiative in collaboration with The Ivors Academy and The Music Rights Awareness Foundation (which he co-founded). The initiative, unveiled at the Ivor Novello awards in September 2021, aims to ensure that complete and accurate song metadata is attached to all recordings at the point of creation.

"The vast amount of new songs released on a daily basis, many with very little metadata and missing linked identifiers causes enormous problems for the music industry. [This is] the reason why so much royalty money each year is unidentified and not paid."
3

Jim Griffin - Managing Director, OneHouse LLC

Email: griffin@onehouse.com

Jim Griffin is the Managing Director of OneHouse LLC, an organization dedicated to the future of music and entertainment delivery. A forward-thinking technologist and industry pioneer, Griffin has been at the forefront of digital music issues for decades. He started and ran the technology department at Geffen Records for five years, where in June 1994, he led a team that distributed the first full-length commercial song online.

"Metadata is the biggest little problem plaguing the music industry."
