Music

Björn Ulvaeus

Role: Songwriter, Musician (ABBA), President of CISAC, Founder of Credits Due Initiative

Twitter: N/A
"The vast amount of new songs released on a daily basis, many with very little metadata and missing linked identifiers causes enormous problems for the music industry. [This is] the reason why so much royalty money each year is unidentified and not paid."
Source: Credits Due Initiative launch, 2021, https://www.nme.com/news/music/abbas-bjorn-ulvaeus-launches-credits-due-campaign-to-fix-music-royalty-issue-3052041
"It's very simple, music recordings must credit all involved and thus ensure that the right people get paid. People ask me why this isn't the case already and I don't know what to say. Today, in 2021, there's really no excuse. If we achieve Credits Due it's a win-win for the whole music industry."
Source: Variety, 2021, https://variety.com/2021/music/news/abba-bjorn-ulveus-credits-due-songwriters-1235070129/

Björn Ulvaeus, best known as a member of ABBA, launched the Credits Due initiative in collaboration with The Ivors Academy and The Music Rights Awareness Foundation (which he co-founded). The initiative, unveiled at the Ivor Novello awards in September 2021, aims to ensure that complete and accurate song metadata is attached to all recordings at the point of creation. Ulvaeus has highlighted that approximately £500 million ($625 million) in royalties is unallocated or misallocated each year worldwide due to missing and incomplete metadata. The Credits Due initiative proposes that all new recordings and songs include five key metadata elements: song titles (including alternative titles), musical work identifiers, creator identifiers and role codes, recording identifiers, and details of all contributors. The problem is growing more acute with 60,000 new tracks added to Spotify daily. Ulvaeus serves as the President of CISAC (International Confederation of Societies of Authors and Composers) and co-founder of The Music Rights Awareness Foundation (MRAF).

Chris McMurtry

Role: Founder of DART (Digital Asset and Repertoire Transfer), Metadata Management Company

Twitter: N/A
"Any digital technology, regardless of the market, is only as good as the data, because that's what we're interacting with. And so if you get the data right, then if it is the system that's bad, then you're at the core of solving that system."
Source: WMOT Interview, 2017, https://www.wmot.org/roots-radio-news/2017-03-20/music-money-and-metadata-dart-aims-at-musics-data-crisis

Chris McMurtry is the founder of DART (Digital Asset and Repertoire Transfer), a company that has developed proprietary software to address the metadata crisis in the music industry. DART's software "collects and collates metadata from hundreds of sources and cleans it up, creating the closest thing the industry has to a definitive ledger of song information." McMurtry has highlighted specific examples of metadata problems in the music industry, such as how a songwriter like Chuck Berry might be registered under different names (Chuck Berry, Charles Berry, and E. Anderson) across various performing rights organizations, creating challenges in connecting these identities to ensure proper attribution and payment. McMurtry's work with DART is described as "step one to fair, accurate and transparent digital commerce" in the music industry. He has emphasized that fixing metadata is at the core of solving systemic problems in the digital music ecosystem.

Photography

David Riecks

Role: Co-Lead, IPTC Photo Metadata Working Group; Founder, ControlledVocabulary.com

Twitter: @ControlledVocab
"The problem is that many of these websites, platforms and social media sites are systematically stripping embedded metadata that provides critical information about the creator of the image, copyright ownership, and contact information, as well as descriptive info about the image content."
Source: ControlledVocabulary.com blog, https://www.controlledvocabulary.com/blog/social-media-metadata-test.html

David Riecks is a professional photographer, digital-image technologist, and metadata evangelist who has dedicated himself to spreading awareness about the value of photo metadata, particularly for long-term preservation. Riecks currently co-leads the development and maintenance of the IPTC Photo Metadata schemas alongside Michael Steidl. As co-leads of the IPTC Photo Metadata Working Group, they regularly provide updates on developments in the field of photo metadata at conferences and events. Riecks has extensive experience in photography standards, having chaired the Digital Photography Standards & Practices committees. He founded ControlledVocabulary.com, which provides resources on image metadata best practices, and leads the Photo Metadata Project for the Stock Artists Alliance. Riecks has conducted extensive testing of how various platforms handle embedded metadata and has advocated for better practices to maintain creator information with digital images. His "Metadata Manifesto" outlines principles for preserving image rights information.

Photography

Michael Steidl

Role: Former Managing Director, IPTC; Co-Lead, IPTC Photo Metadata Working Group

Twitter: @IPTCupdates
"There are many important reasons to embed and preserve metadata – to protect copyrights, ensure proper licensing, track image use, smooth workflow, and make them searchable on- or offline."
Source: IPTC Social Media Platforms Photo Metadata Test, 2016, https://blogs.loc.gov/thesignal/2013/04/social-media-networks-stripping-data-from-your-digital-photos/
"Platforms don't want to pay to include more metadata. They have to save storage space, computing resources, bandwidth, and providing a UI for displaying it is work. We'd like to have more adoption, but the business case isn't there."
Source: IPTC Photo Metadata Conference, 2019, https://iptc.org/news/photo-metadata-conference-2019-shows-our-best-path-forward/

Michael Steidl served as the Managing Director of the International Press Telecommunications Council (IPTC) for 15 years before retiring in the summer of 2018. Under his leadership, the IPTC developed and maintained the IPTC Photo Metadata Standard, the most widely used standard for embedding descriptive, administrative, and copyright information in digital images. Steidl has led numerous initiatives to track and expose metadata handling practices across platforms, including the annual IPTC Social Media Platforms Photo Metadata Test, which evaluates how social media and content platforms handle embedded image metadata. He has been a tireless advocate for improved metadata preservation in the digital ecosystem, highlighting the critical importance of maintaining connection between images and their attribution information. Steidl has emphasized that the stripping of metadata from images by platforms is a significant problem that affects creators' ability to protect their rights and receive proper credit for their work.

Publishing

Brian O'Leary

Role: Executive Director of Book Industry Study Group (BISG)

Twitter: @brianoleary
"In publishing, the quality of metadata directly correlates with discoverability, sales, and long-term value of content. Bad metadata is effectively a tax on publishers that many don't even realize they're paying."
Source: BISG Annual Meeting, 2018, https://bisg.org/news/391106/BISG-Annual-Meeting-Highlights-Supply-Chain-Challenges.htm
"Content without metadata is content that will not be found, seen, distributed, or sold. Period."
Source: Publishing Industry Conference keynote, 2016, https://www.bookbusinessmag.com/post/metadata-matters-making-books-discoverable-digital-age/

Brian O'Leary is the Executive Director of the Book Industry Study Group (BISG), the leading book trade association for standardized best practices, research, and information. Before joining BISG, he founded Magellan Media Consulting, working with publishers on issues related to workflow, content, and metadata. O'Leary has been a leading voice on the importance of standardized, high-quality metadata in publishing, emphasizing how it impacts discoverability, marketing effectiveness, and sales. Under his leadership, BISG has developed and promoted metadata best practices and standards like ONIX for Books, which facilitates the communication of book industry product information in electronic form. He has emphasized that the quality of metadata directly correlates with discoverability, sales, and long-term value of content, describing bad metadata as "a tax on publishers that many don't even realize they're paying." O'Leary's work focuses on helping publishers understand the critical importance of metadata for ensuring their content can be found, seen, distributed, and sold in an increasingly digital marketplace.
