Missing Attribution: How DA1's neverBlank System Prevents Creator Erasure

Dainis W. Michel May 12, 2025 3 min read

When your name gets stripped from your work online, it's not just a technical glitch—it's identity theft by erosion. Creator names get stripped, shortened, or completely lost as content moves through the digital ecosystem, resulting in a crisis of authorship.

"The erasure of creator attribution online has reached epidemic proportions. Our research indicates that by the third generation of sharing, over 85% of creative works have lost all connection to their original creators."

— Sarah Howes, Former Director of Legal Policy, Graphic Artists Guild

The Four Horsemen of Attribution Apocalypse

Missing attribution happens through four primary mechanisms:

  • Metadata stripping during format conversion and platform uploads
  • Incomplete tag fields that don't capture full attribution information
  • User re-uploads without preserving original credits
  • Social sharing that discards embedded identification data

The result is a digital landscape where audiences don't even know who made what. For creators, this means becoming progressively invisible as your work circulates—your connection to your own creation dissolving with every share.

DA1's neverBlank Attribution System

DA1's revolutionary neverBlank system ensures that attribution can never be completely lost:

  • Default fallbacks that activate when primary attribution is stripped
  • Progressive attribution layers that survive even aggressive format conversion
  • Extended context fields capturing the who, what, when, why, where, and how
  • Multiple verification methods that can reconnect orphaned content

Unlike traditional metadata that simply disappears when stripped, DA1's neverBlank system creates redundant, cross-referenced attribution pathways that ensure some connection to the creator always remains intact.

"What makes DA1's approach revolutionary is that it doesn't just add more metadata—it creates an attribution system designed specifically to survive the hostile environment of the modern internet."

— Chase Jarvis, Photographer and Founder, CreativeLive

Beyond Simple Name Tags

The neverBlank system goes far beyond simple creator name tags, supporting a rich attribution ecosystem:

  • Collaborative attribution for team-created works
  • Role-specific credits that recognize diverse contributions
  • Pseudonym support for creators who use various identities
  • Cultural and historical attribution for works based on traditional knowledge
  • Lineage recognition for derivative and remixed works

This comprehensive approach ensures that all forms of creative contribution can be properly recognized and preserved, regardless of how content is shared or transformed.
